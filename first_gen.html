First Generation Student Support at UMich

Around 4,000 students at the University of Michigan are the first in their families to attend college. While this can feel overwhelming, it's also an exciting and important milestone. Fortunately, U-M offers valuable programs and networks to help first-generation students build connections and succeed on campus. This website provides the tools you need to discover resources, find your community, and settle into college life. Make U-M your home and take advantage of these opportunities!

What is a first gen student?

At the University of Michigan, undergraduate and graduate students are first-generation college students if neither of their parents or guardians has earned a four-year college or university degree. This also applies to students with other relatives, such as siblings, cousins, or grandparents, who have completed a four-year degree.

Did you know?

Here are some statistics about first generation students at the University of Michigan:

  • 31% are underrepresented minority students who are first gen.
  • 66% are first gen family households with incomes less than $65,000.
  • 14% of UM undergraduate students are first generation.
  • 50% of first gen students come from ethnic/racial minority backgrounds.
