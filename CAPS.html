Mental Health Support at UMich

At the University of Michigan, we understand that thriving academically involves more than just hitting the books. It's about maintaining a healthy balance between your mind, body, and studies. This is where Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) can play a crucial role in your journey.

What is CAPS?

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) is a comprehensive resource dedicated to supporting the mental health and well-being of U-M students. Our services are tailored to address a range of concerns that you may face during your college experience, from managing stress to dealing with more serious mental health issues.

How can CAPS help you?

CAPS offers a variety of services designed to support your personal and academic growth:

  • Let's Talk: "Let's Talk" is a free program that offers students informal drop-in sessions with counselors. These meetings provide initial support and guidance to students embarking on their mental health journeys.
  • Individual Peer Counseling: IPC is a service aimed at meeting the mental health needs of U-M students. It provides a supportive space where students can talk and express their feelings.
  • UWill Teletherapy Services: Uwill is a teletherapy service that offers video, phone, chat, and message sessions with licensed mental health professionals at no cost to students. This service increases flexibility and expands CAPS's service capacity.
  • First Year Guide: CAPS welcomes first-year students, both on-campus and remote, with "Go Blue! A New Student's Guide to Surviving and Thriving at U-M: Mental Health Tips," a resource designed just for them.
  • Support Regarding the Violence in Israel and Gaza: CAPS extends its support to students affected by the violence in Israel and Gaza. The service acknowledges the emotions students may experience and encourages self-care and community support.
  • Self-Care For Incidents Of Campus Climate Concerns: CAPS addresses how to cope with social injustices and bias incidents on campus, highlighting the importance of self-care, community support, and promoting social justice.
  • Togetherall: Togetherall is an anonymous, online peer-support tool clinically moderated to help students who may find it difficult to connect verbally with CAPS resources. It empowers students to seek and provide support in an anonymous, supportive environment.
  • Sexualized Violence: CAPS addresses sexual violence, which affects individuals regardless of gender, culture, race, or other identities. The service encompasses support for all impacted individuals.
  • Support For Students Impacted By Campus Climate Concerns: CAPS is dedicated to promoting diversity, inclusivity, and mutual respect on campus. The service provides a safe environment for all students and stands firmly against discrimination and harassment.
